<P><FONT size=3>　　 Title: Special Autonomy and Political Neutrality: The Constitutional Implications of "One Country, Two Systems"</FONT></P>
<P><FONT size=3>Keywords: Hong Kong Basic Law, Legal autonomy, Political autonomy, Political neutrality</FONT></P>
<P><FONT size=3>Abstract: The "one-country, two-systems" principle defined in the Sino-British Joint Declaration and the Hong Kong Basic Law has effectively guaranteed the unity of China's sovereignty and the high degree of autonomy in the SAR. Recently, however, the Central Government and the Hong Kong public opinion differed on the scenario of the Hong Kong democratic reform. The key to resolve the puzzle that arises from the Hong Kong political reform still lies in the Basic Law principle of "one-country, two-systems" itself. As long as the Central Government maintains a neutral political position, acquire good will and mutual trust from various sections of the Hong Kong public, confides in the rationality and wisdom of the Hong Kong electorates, and establish a electoral scheme based on "one-person, one-vote", Hong Kong will be blessed with peace and stability; meanwhile, the authority of the Central Government and the unity of China's sovereignty will be well respected.</FONT></P>
